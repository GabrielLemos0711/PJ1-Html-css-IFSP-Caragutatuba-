A Little More About the Sea

The ocean is currently the final repository for large amounts of toxic waste and sewage. Modern society, especially Western society, is characterized by the massive production of non-biodegradable waste, of which plastics stand out, floating in the waters of the entire planet or deposited at the bottom. These objects remain in the environment for long periods of time, in some cases centuries, before being decomposed by natural processes. Basically, they cause dramatic changes in the physical properties of the environment and bury the flora and fauna. They can also be mistaken for food by fish and eventually die after being eaten. Microplastics are already present throughout the marine food chain, taken up by microbes and then transmitted to larger organisms.
